Web Security

A non-comprehensive primer

November 22, 2015

How do I stop being afraid? Know that there is no safety anywhere. There never was and there never will be. Stop looking for it. Live with a fierce intent to waste nothing of yourself or life.
Ann Shulgin

For this week's blog post, we were encouraged to go deeper in our research, and learn about a topic beyond the scope of our current studies. Even with that caveat in mind, I think I bit off more than I can chew with this topic. Nevertheless, I will provide a high-level overview of some of the main issues around web security.

It's dangerous out there.

The woods are dark and deep. There are malicious people (and the programs they have created) on the internet, and there are myriad ways that an evil-minded person can violate the security of your web site.

Are the bad guys out to get me?

The good news is that not everyone has to worry. Security on the web is a relative thing, and the content of your site is a major factor. If you fit one or more of the following categories, you should be very concerned with your security:

  • Is your data valuable? to others? (eg credit card information, personal data, etc).
  • Is your site controversial in any way? The web is a popular place for activists of all stripes to make their mark without getting out of their chairs.
  • Are you (or your site) just plain famous? Visbility = vulnerability, just like IRL.

Vulnerability vs Interactivity

There is a direct relation to the amount of input you allow from users of your site, to the damage one of those users may cause. A site that is a completely non-interactive is highly secure, but less useful; while an interactive site allows far more opportunities for bad actors.

What could happen?

Here is a short list of some of the major attacks that can occur over the web:

  • Cross Site Scripting (XSS) attacks will execute a script in a victim computer's browser.
  • Injection attacks (which are typically exploted through SQL) occur when hositle data is used to trick a database interpreter into executing unwanted commands, or changing data.
  • Malicious file execution is when an attacked inserts code that can later compromise the system. Consequences can be as serious as total server compromise.

Who do I have to pay to be secure?

First, the bad news: there is no instant, complete way to become secure on the web. Just as no suit of armor is completely invulnerable, there will always be a new way to crack into a web site.

One bit of good news is that amongst the range of attacks made on web sites, very few are new. Most often, attackers will use known techniques, only only rarely will a new technique emerge. This means that if you can stay up-to-date with current security protocols (a topic out of reach of this post), you can re-attain a high level of security.

While this was so very far from a complete coverage of this topic, I still hope you learned something. Thanks for reading, and come back around next week!.
