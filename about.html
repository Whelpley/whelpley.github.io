How a doge learns to code

A short biography

I grew up in the great state of New Jersey, youngest of eight children. My parents worked hard and were able to send me to Delbarton, a fine institution of learning. Aside attaining a solid base of Western education, I began developing my somatic skills in soccer, kung fu, juggling, and cross-country running.

From there, I was able to secure an admission to Princeton University, where I studied Electrical Engineering, making my first deep dive into technical studies. Along the way, I learned capoeira & break-dancing, hosted a weekly show on WPRB, and wrote, edited, & planed pranks for the venerable Tiger Magazine.

Following graduation, I initially pursued a career in intellectual property law, first doing research for a local attorney, then moving to Virginia to put in a year examining invention applications at the US Patent and Trademark Office.

It turned out that the IP field was not quite my cup of tea, so after my time there I decided take a radical turn with my career: I moved to San Francisco, and spent a decade working in the culinary field.

As those ten years rounded up, I decided to make another major life change, and re-embrace the tech world. I chose to enter the Dev Bootcamp program because it looked to be an excellent way to bring me back up to speed with the current state of web technology.

Dev Bootcamp is putting my brain through the wringer, but each week I feel a little stronger. Sometimes, I play "Eye of the Tiger" in my head and imagine this all as a movie training montage. It helps, don't judge.
